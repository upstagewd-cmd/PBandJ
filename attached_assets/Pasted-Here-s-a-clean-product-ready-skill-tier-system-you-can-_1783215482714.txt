Here’s a clean, product-ready skill tier system you can plug directly into Base44. This is designed to be simple for users, but still meaningful for matchmaking and your hidden rating system.

⸻

🧠 SKILL LEVEL SYSTEM (PB&J)

These are self-selected starting tiers that seed the hidden rating system. They are NOT permanent skill labels — performance will naturally adjust via rating over time.

⸻

🟢 BEGINNER

Starting Rating:

1000

Description:

Players who are new to pickleball or still developing consistent control, positioning, and strategy.

Typical characteristics:
	•	Inconsistent serves and returns
	•	Still learning court positioning (especially kitchen rules)
	•	Rallies are often short
	•	Relies more on reaction than strategy
	•	Limited experience with structured doubles play

What this means in the system:
	•	Treated as a developing baseline player
	•	When paired, system tries to balance them with stronger partners
	•	Gains rating faster from wins vs stronger opponents (to help normalization)

⸻

🔵 INTERMEDIATE

Starting Rating:

1500

Description:

Players who are comfortable with doubles play and understand core strategy, spacing, and shot selection.

Typical characteristics:
	•	Consistent serve and return
	•	Understands kitchen strategy and dinking
	•	Can sustain rallies and build points
	•	Basic team communication and positioning
	•	Can occasionally pressure advanced players

What this means in the system:
	•	Default “balanced competitive” level
	•	Most matches should aim to center around this tier
	•	Used heavily in matchmaking balancing logic

⸻

🔴 ADVANCED

Starting Rating:

2000

Description:

Experienced, high-skill players with strong control, anticipation, and competitive consistency.

Typical characteristics:
	•	Strong serve + aggressive return game
	•	Comfortable with fast hands at the net
	•	Consistently strategic shot selection
	•	Strong court awareness and positioning
	•	Can control tempo of the game

What this means in the system:
	•	System avoids pairing Advanced + Advanced when possible (to reduce blowouts)
	•	Advanced players lose/gain rating more slowly against lower tiers (to stabilize system)
	•	Primary “anchor players” for balancing games fairly

⸻

🧠 HOW THESE TIERS WORK IN YOUR SYSTEM

1. They are ONLY starting points

Every player quickly diverges based on performance.

⸻

2. Hidden Rating takes over immediately

After ~5–10 matches:
	•	Beginner can become Intermediate-equivalent
	•	Intermediate can become Advanced-equivalent
	•	Advanced can drop if overestimated

⸻

3. Used for initial matchmaking only

Before rating stabilizes:
	•	Beginner + Advanced pairing is preferred for balance
	•	Intermediate acts as the “bridge tier”

⸻

⚙️ MATCHMAKING LOGIC (SIMPLE RULE)

When forming teams:

Step 1:

Convert skill → rating seed:
	•	Beginner = 1000
	•	Intermediate = 1500
	•	Advanced = 2000

Step 2:

Use hidden rating if available (more than ~5 matches)

Step 3:

Balance teams by:
	•	Minimizing total team rating difference
	•	Avoiding extreme same-tier stacking when possible

⸻

🧭 DESIGN INTENT

This system is NOT meant to label players permanently.

Instead it:

“Gets you started in the right neighborhood, then lets your play define your real level.”

⸻

	•	fixed ranking system

They are ONLY:
	•	onboarding classification
	•	matchmaking seed
